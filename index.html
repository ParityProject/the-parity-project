Flagship Dispatch

The Healthcare Ledger: What Extraction Costs You on the Clock

Re-centering universal healthcare from a moral claim to fixed capital recovery and public infrastructure.

The Hourly Cost of the Extraction Machine

When you add what gets docked from a family pay stub ($6,850/yr) to what employers are forced to pay on the back end to keep group health plans alive ($20,143/yr), the total corporate health tax comes out to nearly $13.00 for every single hour worked on the clock.

That isn't a benefit. It is an unauthorized private tax siphoned straight out of your labor and handed over to an insurance cartel that produces nothing.

“So let me ask you a straight question: What would an extra thirteen dollars an hour do for your life? Medicare for All isn't a government handout. It’s a wage recovery act.”
Key Structural Pivot

Treating single-payer healthcare not as "government-run medicine," but as basic public infrastructure—just like the interstate highway system. It's a public right-of-way that ensures commerce and human life move without private tollbooths or prior authorizations.
